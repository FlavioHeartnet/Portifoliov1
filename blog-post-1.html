Why Knowledge Workers are so important to organizations

Now let's talk about this new type of professional, although new is a term only used by this type of professional is in high demand in the market and we have few available, but the term was created in 1959 by professor and writer Peter Drucker and is used to define professionals who have the ability to act on the knowledge of a given area and communicate it, they will advance their knowledge as they evolve in the subject they are working on and use this capacity to define problems and identify alternatives to solve them, they work to solve problems, influence decisions, priorities and strategies of any project.

Knowledge workers would be the most valuable assets of a 21st century organization because of their high level of productivity, different mindset and creativity.
Peter Drucker

The advantage of working with this mindset is that you can break big problems into smaller ones and look for solutions that solve these problems through research, study and development, not only about having the right skills for such an activity but looking for what you need to solve the challenges ahead, emotionally speaking it removes many insecurities related to job delivery, meeting deadlines or being accountable to superiors or investors, because you will always have something that minimally solves the problem, helps your motivation, because you can see it first hand where your project is taking you, each small problem solved you arrive at a new place and mainly you have freedom and options for professional activities, not only limited to your college curriculum, you can act in any area as long as it is your will and of course , now as a home activity I want you to write on a paper what skills you have to are characteristics of a knowledge worker and which ones you need to develop.
